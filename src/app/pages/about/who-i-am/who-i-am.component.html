Who I am

I am Phani Narra, a software engineer, passionate about using technology to help you become a better version of yourself.

I believe and live by the following principle

There are `some` things you know (knowns), and
there are `some` things you know that you dont know (known unknowns), and
there are `many` things you dont know but you dont even know that you dont know (unknown unknowns).



If you spend most of your day doing your (knowns) then you are not challenging yourself enough in life. The more time you spend learning (known unknowns), the more of (unknown unknowns) you uncover, and the more knowledgable you become.



This website was built to help you uncover your (unknown unknowns).

Phani Narra (Original Knowns and Unknowns concept by Donald Rumsfield)

I am an IT professional and have been in IT since 2010 and did my masters and bachelors degrees in computer science before that. I came across the knowns and unknowns mantra I mentioned above in the year 2009. I was a student and doing my masters back then, and the thing just stuck in my mind eventhough I did not completely understand the depth of the meaning it carries. Later somewhere around the 2014 time, I experienced something extraordinary in my life that I was able to directly apply this mantra to, which is when I truly understood the depth of the meaning and realized that we need to spend most of our time everyday trying to learn things in the known unknowns space. This essentially does two things:

  1. Adds new things to your knowns space from your known unknowns space.
  2. Adds new things to your known unknowns space from your unknown unknowns space.

and the cycle continues everyday.
